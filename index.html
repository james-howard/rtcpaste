Remote Video:

This page demonstrates a WebKit bug on iOS where receiving video via WebRTC is blocked by the clipboard UI, leading to memory growth (video frames keep getting received and decoded into IOSurfaces, but they never get consumed / released until the paste UI is dismissed).

Usage:
Have one Mac with a webcam and one iOS device (running iOS 14.4 or later) on the same local network. Or one Mac running the iOS Simulator works too.

  1. Setup and Signaling
    1. From the Mac side, open this page in Safari and click the "Be Sender" button. Agree to let the page access your camera.
    2. From the iOS side, open this page in Safari and tap the "Be Receiver" button.
    3. From the Mac side, copy the offer text and paste it into the iOS offer form.
      • Tip: Use Universal Clipboard
    4. From the iOS side, copy the answer text and paste it into the Mac answer form.
    5. A video should appear on the iOS side, streamed from the Mac.
  2. Use Instruments "Activity Monitor" to track memory usage on the iOS device (or simulator)
  3. From the iOS side, tap on the "Paste" button again, but don't yet tap on the "Paste" callout that appears, just let it sit there.
  4. Watch the memory use grow!
